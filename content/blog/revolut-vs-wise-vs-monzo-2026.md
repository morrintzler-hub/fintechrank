Revolut, Wise, and Monzo together serve more than 80 million customers across Europe and beyond. They are regularly recommended in the same breath, but they were built for different purposes and remain different products in 2026.

## Quick verdict

**Choose Revolut** if you want one app for everyday banking, currency exchange, crypto, stock trading, and travel.

**Choose Wise** if you regularly send money internationally, receive payments in foreign currencies, or hold balances in multiple currencies.

**Choose Monzo** if you want the best everyday current account for UK spending, the best budgeting tools, and the most responsive customer support of any bank in the UK.

Most financially active people end up using two of the three. The most common combination is Monzo for everyday UK banking and Wise for international transfers.

## How they started

**Wise** launched in 2011 as TransferWise, founded by two Estonians frustrated by hidden fees on international bank transfers. The company was built around one obsession: giving individuals the mid-market exchange rate with a transparent fee on top. Everything Wise has built since traces back to that origin.

**Monzo** launched in 2015 as a prepaid card and became a fully licensed UK bank in 2017. It was built as a better current account with faster notifications, smarter budgeting tools, and customer support that actually responds.

**Revolut** launched in 2015 as a travel card that eliminated foreign transaction fees. It has since expanded aggressively into crypto, stock trading, insurance, business banking, and savings. No other European fintech covers as many financial verticals in one app.

## Exchange rates: where the real money is

**Wise** always uses the mid-market rate for currency conversion. You pay a transparent fee of typically 0.41% to 0.6% on top of that rate, disclosed before you confirm the transfer. On a £1,000 transfer to euros, you might pay £5 in fees and receive exactly what the mid-market rate says you should.

**Revolut** uses the interbank rate on weekday transfers during market hours. On weekends, Revolut adds a markup of up to 1%. On the free plan, there is a fair usage limit of £1,000 per month at the interbank rate, with a 0.5% fee above that.

**Monzo** routes international transfers through Wise, which means you get Wise-level rates via Monzo's interface with no additional markup.

The practical implication: for international transfers, Wise is cheapest in most scenarios. For infrequent transfers under £1,000 on weekdays, Revolut on the free plan is competitive.

## Everyday UK banking

For day-to-day spending in the UK, Monzo is the strongest product. Its budgeting features — spending categories, custom limits, salary sorter, savings pots, round-ups — are more sophisticated and intuitive than either competitor.

Monzo's customer support is available 24/7 via in-app chat and consistently ranks as the best of any bank in the UK in independent surveys.

Monzo holds a full UK banking licence, meaning deposits up to £85,000 are FSCS protected — the same protection as any high street bank.

Revolut received its UK banking licence in 2024, meaning UK deposits are now also FSCS protected. Its everyday banking features have improved significantly but it still feels more like a fintech application than a bank account.

Wise is not really a current account. It is a multi-currency account optimised for holding and sending money internationally.

## Currency accounts

**Wise** supports 50+ currencies and provides local bank account details in 10 markets including USD, EUR, GBP, AUD, and SGD. This means you can give a US client a real US account number, receive the funds at the mid-market rate, and hold the balance in dollars. For freelancers and small businesses with international clients, this is transformative.

**Revolut** holds 36 currencies and offers local IBANs in a smaller set of markets.

**Monzo** is GBP only for account balances, with no multi-currency account offering.

## Pricing summary

| | Free plan | Paid plans |
|---|---|---|
| Monzo | Full current account | Plus £5/mo, Premium £15/mo |
| Wise | Free account, pay per transfer | No subscription |
| Revolut | Limited FX allowance | Plus £2.99, Premium £6.99, Metal £12.99/mo |

Wise has no monthly subscription — you only pay when you use it. This makes it most cost-effective for infrequent users who primarily want good FX rates.

## The combination most people end up using

The most common optimal setup in 2026 is Monzo as your primary UK current account and Wise for international transfers and foreign currency balances. Revolut earns its place for frequent travellers who want crypto and travel perks in one app and are willing to pay for a premium plan.

The worst outcome is committing to one app and expecting it to be best at everything. None of them are.

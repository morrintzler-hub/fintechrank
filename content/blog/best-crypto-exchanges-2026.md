Choosing a crypto exchange in 2026 involves navigating a more complex landscape than most beginners expect. The exchange with the lowest fees is rarely the most secure. The most heavily regulated exchange rarely has the widest coin selection. This guide compares the five largest global exchanges on the metrics that actually matter.

## Quick verdict

**Coinbase** — Best for beginners in the US and UK who prioritise regulatory protection over fees.

**Kraken** — Best for US and European users who want strong regulation at lower fees than Coinbase.

**Binance** — Best for experienced traders outside the US wanting maximum liquidity and coin selection.

**Bybit** — Best for active derivatives traders outside the US wanting deep futures liquidity.

**OKX** — Best for traders outside the US wanting broad altcoin selection with competitive fees.

## Regulatory standing

**Coinbase** is publicly listed on Nasdaq, making it one of the most accountable crypto companies in the world. It is licensed in all 50 US states and holds an Electronic Money Institution licence in the UK.

**Kraken** was founded in 2011 and has never been hacked at the exchange level. It holds money transmission licences across the US and multiple European jurisdictions.

**Binance** reached a $4.3 billion settlement with US regulators in 2023 — the largest crypto enforcement action in history. Its founder pleaded guilty to AML violations and stepped down as CEO. Binance is not available to US users through its main platform and operates under lighter-touch regulation in most markets where it does business.

**Bybit** is registered in Dubai under the VARA framework. It does not serve US customers and has a shorter track record than Coinbase and Kraken.

**OKX** is registered in Seychelles and holds licences in a growing number of jurisdictions. Like Bybit, it does not serve US customers.

## Fees compared

**Coinbase** via the standard app costs 1.49% to 3.99% depending on payment method. Using Coinbase Advanced Trade drops this to 0.6%/1.2% maker/taker at standard volumes. If you are buying on Coinbase and not using Advanced Trade, you are paying significantly more than necessary.

**Kraken** charges 0.25% maker / 0.40% taker at standard volumes — materially lower than Coinbase.

**Binance** charges 0.1% maker / 0.1% taker at standard rates, dropping further with BNB token holdings.

**Bybit** charges 0.1%/0.1% for spot and 0.02%/0.055% for perpetual futures.

**OKX** charges 0.08%/0.1% for spot trading — among the lowest of any major exchange.

For a trader doing $10,000 in monthly volume, the annual fee difference between Coinbase standard and Binance/OKX can exceed $1,500. For long-term holders making occasional purchases, the regulatory safety of Coinbase or Kraken is worth the premium.

## Coin selection

**Bybit** lists 1,100+ trading pairs, the widest in this comparison.

**Binance** and **OKX** each list 350+ coins with deep liquidity.

**Kraken** lists approximately 200 coins, curated for regulatory standing and established track records.

**Coinbase** lists approximately 240 coins, applying relatively stringent standards that filter out the most speculative assets.

For Bitcoin, Ethereum, and the top 20 coins by market cap, every exchange has adequate liquidity. Coin selection differences matter when trading assets ranked below the top 50.

## Security track record

**Coinbase** and **Kraken** have never suffered a major exchange-level hack. Coinbase holds FDIC pass-through insurance for USD balances up to $250,000.

**Binance** maintains a SAFU fund of approximately $1 billion to cover losses. It experienced a $40 million exchange hack in 2019 which was covered by SAFU.

**Bybit** and **OKX** maintain proof-of-reserves systems where independent auditors verify customer assets are fully backed.

If you are based in the US, your practical options are Coinbase and Kraken. Everything else involves workarounds that create legal and financial risk.

## Our recommendation by user type

**New to crypto, US or UK:** Start with Coinbase. Use Advanced Trade from day one to avoid high standard app fees. Regulatory protection and ease of use are worth the modest premium.

**Cost-conscious, US or UK:** Kraken. Lower fees than Coinbase, equally strong regulatory standing, 15-year track record.

**Active trader outside the US:** Binance or OKX for spot trading, Bybit for derivatives. Fee savings are material at any significant volume.

**Long-term Bitcoin holder:** Coinbase or Kraken, then move holdings to a hardware wallet. Never hold large amounts on any exchange long-term regardless of its reputation.

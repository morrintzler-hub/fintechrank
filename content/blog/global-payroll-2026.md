Global payroll was once a problem only enterprise companies with dedicated legal teams had to solve. In 2026, with remote work normalised and talent markets global, teams of ten are regularly paying contractors across five countries and hiring full-time employees in markets where they have no legal entity.

Deel, Gusto, and Rippling have each built serious products for this problem, from very different starting points.

## Quick verdict

**Choose Deel** if you are hiring international contractors or full-time employees in countries where you have no legal entity. Its Employer of Record service is the most comprehensive in the market.

**Choose Gusto** if you are a US-based business with primarily domestic employees wanting the simplest, most complete payroll and HR product at transparent pricing.

**Choose Rippling** if you want to consolidate payroll, HR, IT device management, and spend management into one connected platform.

## What each was built to solve

**Deel** was founded in 2019 specifically to solve international hiring compliance. Before Deel, hiring a full-time employee in Germany without a German legal entity meant setting up a subsidiary (months of work, thousands in legal fees) or using a local employer of record with opaque pricing. Deel built EOR infrastructure covering 150+ countries, handling local contracts, tax filings, and statutory benefits through a single platform with standardised pricing.

**Gusto** was founded in 2011 as ZenPayroll, focused on making US payroll simple enough for a non-HR founder to run without a payroll specialist. It expanded into full HR — benefits administration, onboarding, performance reviews, time tracking — while maintaining ease of use. Gusto processes payroll for over 300,000 US businesses.

**Rippling** was founded in 2016 with the thesis that HR, IT, and Finance are really one problem: managing people and everything connected to them. Its distinguishing feature is its compound architecture — every module shares the same employee record, so onboarding a new hire can simultaneously create their Slack account, order their MacBook, add them to the correct projects, and set up their payroll, all from one workflow.

## International hiring

**Deel** covers 150+ countries for both contractor payments and EOR full-time employment. The EOR service handles local employment contracts, monthly payroll in local currency, tax withholding and filing, statutory benefits, and equity management on higher tiers.

EOR pricing starts at $599 per employee per month. A team with five international full-time employees via Deel costs roughly $3,000/month in Deel fees before salaries. For companies that have modelled the alternative (local entities, local legal fees, dedicated HR staff per country), $599/month is often cheaper.

Deel's contractor management is priced at $49/month per contractor — far more accessible for companies starting with freelance relationships.

**Rippling** has built global payroll covering 50+ countries. Integration with the broader Rippling platform is tighter than Deel can offer — payroll feeds automatically into accounting, equity, and benefits.

**Gusto** covers the US and Canada. It is not the right choice if international hiring is your primary requirement.

## Ease of use

**Gusto** consistently earns the highest ease-of-use ratings. Founders without HR backgrounds run it without a payroll specialist. The onboarding process is clean and the support team is knowledgeable about US compliance nuances.

**Deel** has improved significantly. The contractor onboarding flow is smooth — a new contractor receives a link, uploads their details, and is set up within hours. EOR onboarding involves more documentation by necessity but the platform guides you through it.

**Rippling** is the most powerful and the most complex. Companies that invest in setup benefit from substantial automation. Companies that do not configure it properly find it as complex as legacy enterprise HR software.

## The combination that works

Many scaling companies end up using Gusto or Rippling for US payroll and domestic HR, with Deel layered on for international contractors and EOR hires. This gives the ease of use of Gusto or power of Rippling for the home market, with Deel's compliance depth for international complexity.

## Bottom line

**US-only team under 50 people:** Gusto. Affordable, easy to configure, handles payroll through onboarding in one clean product.

**Team with international contractors or employees:** Deel. Purpose-built for this problem. The compliance coverage is the deepest available and often cheaper than setting up local entities.

**Company wanting one system for HR, IT, and Finance:** Rippling, but only if you have someone to configure it properly. The power is real. So is the complexity.

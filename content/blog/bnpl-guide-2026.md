Buy now, pay later processed over $300 billion in global transaction volume in 2025. It is now offered at checkout by most major retailers, embedded into credit cards, and available as a standalone app. Klarna, Afterpay, Affirm, and Sezzle handle the majority of this volume, but they operate very differently under the hood.

## How BNPL actually works

The provider pays the retailer immediately. The consumer repays in instalments. The retailer pays the BNPL company a merchant fee of typically 2-8% in exchange for higher conversion rates and larger average order values. The consumer pays nothing if they repay on time.

BNPL companies make money from two sources: merchant fees and consumer late fees. The merchant fees are not your problem as a consumer. The late fees are.

## The four products compared

**Klarna** is the largest BNPL provider globally with 150 million users across 45 countries. It offers Pay in 30 days, Pay in 3, and Klarna Financing. The first two charge no interest. Late fees are capped at £5 per missed payment in the UK. Klarna's strength is retailer coverage — it integrates with more merchants than any competitor.

**Afterpay** (Clearpay in the UK) uses a strict pay-in-4 model: four equal fortnightly payments, no interest, ever. Late fees are capped at 25% of the original order value, and Afterpay pauses your account if you miss a payment rather than continuing to charge fees on a running balance. This capping mechanism is meaningful consumer protection.

**Affirm** offers financing terms of 1 to 36 months with a fixed APR disclosed before you confirm the purchase. APR ranges from 0% at participating merchants to 36% depending on your credit profile. Affirm does not charge late fees — instead, it reports missed payments to credit bureaus, which affects your credit score directly.

**Sezzle** uses the standard pay-in-4 model with one key differentiator: it reports positive payment history to credit bureaus, positioning itself as a tool for building credit.

## Late fee comparison

| Provider | Late fee | Cap | Account suspended? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Klarna | £5 per missed payment | Per payment | No |
| Afterpay | Up to 25% of order | Yes | Yes |
| Affirm | None | None | Credit bureau report |
| Sezzle | $10 per reschedule | $15 per order | Yes |

The critical difference: Afterpay's account suspension on missed payments is actually consumer-friendly. It prevents you accumulating debt across multiple orders while in repayment difficulty. Klarna's per-payment fees can compound if you have multiple active orders.

Affirm's no-late-fee policy sounds generous, but a missed payment on your credit report can have far greater financial consequences than a £5 fee.

## Credit checks and credit impact

**Klarna** and **Afterpay** perform soft credit checks for pay-in-4 and pay-in-30 products, which do not affect your credit score.

**Affirm** always performs a hard credit check. Multiple Affirm applications in a short period can reduce your credit score.

**Sezzle** performs a soft check and uniquely reports positive payment history to Experian as part of its credit-building programme — the only standard BNPL provider that actively helps build credit through on-time payments.

## When BNPL makes financial sense

BNPL is a cash flow tool, not a discount. It makes sense when:

- You have the money now but prefer to spread the payment without using a credit card
- You are buying something with a genuine 0% offer from Affirm where the retailer subsidises the financing
- You need to make an essential purchase and the alternative is a higher-cost credit product

It does not make sense when:

- You are using it to buy things you cannot afford
- You have multiple active BNPL orders across different providers
- You are carrying a balance on a credit card charging 20%+ APR

## The verdict

For straightforward split payments on purchases you can afford, **Afterpay** is the lowest-risk option: simple structure, capped late fees, automatic account suspension that prevents debt accumulation.

For larger purchases needing genuine financing over months, **Affirm's** transparent APR and no late fee policy make it the most honest product — just be aware missed payments affect your credit score.

**Klarna** is the most ubiquitous and flexible, but its multiple product types require you to pay attention to which one you are using at checkout.

**Sezzle** is a niche choice with a specific value: building credit history through BNPL use.

Whatever you choose, treat BNPL as a budgeting tool for purchases you have already decided to make, not a mechanism for purchasing things outside your budget.
